The Life of a College Student

Every year, thousands of young students go off to the college of their choice after finishing long years of high school. Little do they know that their next four years of education will be the most challenging yet best rewarding experience in the long run...

What a student gains from the College Experience

  • A superb education which could eventually result in a higher-level job after college
  • New friends and fellow students who you will spend your college years with
  • The concept of self-reliance and the ability to thrive on your own

The College Experience Beyond Studying

Studying and going to class are a fundamental part of college, however there are many other fun activities college has to offer. On most college campuses, there are clubs, fraternities/sororities, and other fun events offered throughout the school year. Clubs, fraternities, and sororities are a great way to interact with other students and meet new friends. Watching sporting events with fellow colleagues is a very popular pastime that many students enjoy every year. Tailgates with food and drinks before a football game makes the experience one of a kind. One of the great activities offered on college campuses is intramural sports. It allows college students to play the sport of their choosing at a fairly competitive level. All of these activities are an excellent ways to get involved and meet new people!

Opportunities College Provides For Your Career

After college, most students begin a career in a field based on their major. The great thing about college is the freedom to choose your major. Regardless of which major you choose, a college degree will ultimately benefit your career significantly! According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the weekly earnings in 2012 for someone with a Bachelor's Degree was $1,066. The median weekly salary of an individual with only a high school diploma was $652, which is significantly lower in the long run. Another benefit of obtaining a college degree is the increased number of career options. This allows for a lot more freedom in the job of your choice and can open doors to different aspects of your career field. These are just a few of the opportunities a college degree provides for your future career.

How To Stay Organized In College

Juggling projects, tests, homework, essays, and quizzes can be a very difficult task in college. That's why it's important to remain organized and develop a concise schedule to accomplish all of your intended goals. Tests and quizzes should always be prepared for in advance and never the night before. Both of them combined make up a large percentage of your final grade and it is important that you do well on them. Completing your homework on time is crucial, however the most important part is understanding the material assigned in the homework. Another huge assignment in college are essays. Always develop a rough draft beforehand and revise several times before turning it in. This is just a rough foundation guide for succeeding in college, the rest of the process relies on a student applying themselves and keeping an organized schedule to complete all the tasks they need to accomplish.
